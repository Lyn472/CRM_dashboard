        FROM DATA TO DECISIONS: A POWER BI DASHBOARD FOR SALES KPI TRACKING

**1. Overview**

Maven is a B2B computer hardware company that recently adopted a Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system to streamline sales operations.
Despite the implementation, the company has yet to fully leverage the CRM’s analytical capabilities to monitor and optimize sales team performance.

The objective of this project is to develop an interactive Power BI dashboard that:

        - Provides the executive team with real-time visibility into company-wide sales performance, and

        - Enables sales managers to evaluate their teams’ contributions toward organizational KPIs and monitor progress over time.

**2. Dataset**

The dataset comprises CRM exports from October 2016 to December 2017, containing detailed information about sales opportunities, including:

        - Product and account information

        - Opportunity stage (e.g., prospecting, engaging, won, lost)

These records serve as the foundation for building insights into both company-level and team-level performance.

**3. Thought Process**

Before developing any dashboard, I begin by clarifying stakeholder requirements—specifically, identifying the metrics and business questions most relevant to their decision-making.

In this project, since stakeholder input was not directly available, I inferred key analytical questions based on prior experience collaborating with executive leaders and sales management teams.

**Executive-Level Focus**

Executives are primarily interested in understanding the company’s overall revenue health and identifying opportunities for growth.
Typical questions include:

        - Which teams are performing best or underperforming? Do any require additional support?

        - Which geographic regions or markets represent the strongest sales potential?

        - Which industries or client segments contribute most to revenue, and where should relationship-building initiatives be concentrated?

**Sales Manager Focus**

Sales managers are more concerned with their teams’ performance and goal alignment. Key questions include:

        - How is my team performing relative to company KPIs?

        - How does my team’s performance compare to other teams?

        - Which sales representatives are excelling or struggling, and where is additional coaching needed?

        - Which opportunities, products, or clients should we prioritize for the remainder of the quarter?

**4. Metrics**

The company’s primary KPI is total revenue.
To provide a more comprehensive performance overview, I incorporated supporting metrics such as:

        - Number of closed sales

        - Average deal size

        - Conversion time (sales cycle duration)

        - Average discount offered

All these metrics are consolidated into a single-page executive view, enabling both senior leaders and managers to quickly assess business health and contextualize team performance.

**5. Dashboard Design**

A key principle in dashboard design is clarity of purpose—users should immediately understand what the dashboard shows and how to interpret the data.
To support this, I included contextual notes on each page explaining metric definitions and calculation logic, ensuring users can confidently navigate and analyze insights.

The dashboard consists of four main sections:

        - Executive Summary – High-level company KPIs and revenue trends

        - Team Performance – Comparison of team achievements and workload balance

        - Product Analysis – Insights into product-level sales and profitability

        - Sales Pipeline – Visualization of deal stages and conversion efficiency
